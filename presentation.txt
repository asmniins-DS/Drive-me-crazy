prediction approaches on the PeMS04, PeMS07, PeMS08,
and NYCTaxi datasets:

TRADITIONAL MODEL: LSTM
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
Architecture:
  - 2-layer stacked LSTM
  - Hidden size: 64
  - Dropout: 0.1
  - Prediction head: FC layer
  - Input: 12 timesteps (60 min)
  - Output: 12 timesteps (60 min ahead)

Training:
  - Optimizer: Adam (lr=1e-3)
  - Scheduler: StepLR (step=10, Оі=0.5)
  - Loss: MSE
  - Epochs: 30, Batch: 64

Strengths:
  + Simple and fast to train
  + Captures temporal sequences well
  + Low memory requirements

Weaknesses:
  - No spatial modeling (sensors independent)
  - No propagation delay awareness
  - Limited long-range temporal dependency


PDFORMER MODEL
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
Architecture:
  - Stacked PDFormer encoder layers (N=2)
  - d_model=64, n_heads=4
  - Dynamic Spatial Attention (DSA) with learned
    propagation delay bias per sensor pair
  - Temporal Self-Attention (multi-head, per sensor)
  - Feed-Forward Network (GELU, 4Г— expansion)
  - Positional embeddings (temporal)
  - Input: 12 timesteps вҶ’ Output: 12 timesteps

Key Innovation вҖ” Propagation Delay Awareness:
  Traffic propagation is NOT instantaneous. When flow
  increases at sensor A, sensor B (downstream) sees
  the effect after a delay proportional to distance
  and speed. PDFormer models this via a learned (NГ—N)
  delay bias matrix injected into spatial attention.
  This enables the model to correctly attribute
  historical upstream conditions to future downstream
  predictions.

Training:
  - Optimizer: Adam (lr=5e-4, weight_decay=1e-4)
  - Scheduler: CosineAnnealingLR
  - Loss: Huber (robust to outliers)
  - Epochs: 30, Batch: 32

PROPAGATION DELAY ANALYSIS
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
The learned (NГ—N) delay bias matrices reveal:

  1. Asymmetric relationships: AвҶ’B delay вү  BвҶ’A delay,
     consistent with real one-way traffic flows.

  2. Cluster structure: Sensors in the same road segment
     show strong positive mutual bias.

  3. Long-range patterns: Highways show propagation chains
     where upstream sensor bias cascades downstream.

  4. Impact on accuracy: Ablation studies from the original
     paper show ~8вҖ“12% MAE improvement from delay modeling
     alone vs. a transformer without delay awareness.


TRANSFORMER EFFICIENCY ANALYSIS
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
PDFormer captures both:

  Spatial dependencies:
    - Dynamic attention adapts per-timestep (not fixed graph)
    - Sensors are jointly modeled (not independent like LSTM)
    - Outperforms GCN-based methods on heterogeneous networks

  Temporal dependencies:
    - Global attention covers the full sequence length
    - Captures both short-term and long-range patterns
    - Cosine scheduler prevents overfitting

  Across datasets:
    - More scalable on large sensor networks (PeMS07: 883 sensors)
    - Consistent gains across highway (PeMS) and urban (NYC) scenarios
    - NYC taxi: irregular grid benefits from dynamic (not fixed) graph

CONCLUSIONS
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
  1. PDFormer significantly improves traffic flow prediction
     over traditional LSTM baselines across all datasets.

  2. The propagation delay mechanism is the key differentiator,
     especially for longer forecast horizons and dense networks.

  3. Dynamic spatial attention outperforms static graph approaches
     in heterogeneous urban environments (NYCTaxi vs PeMS).

  4. Future work: incorporate weather features, incidents, and
     calendar events as additional input channels.
